TEACHING

Teaching Philosophy

Maria Montessori reminds us that “it is not enough for the teacher to love the child. She must first love and understand the universe.” I am infinitely curious about the way cultures, languages, history, and society shape the educational enterprise, and make it a point to explore issues in education and the world around us with my students. For example, in courses preparing students to work with multilingual learners, we view films such as Rabbit Proof Fence, The Graduates, and Precious Knowledge in order to explore historical and contemporary issues surrounding multilingualism. We also visit community sites of learning, attending PTA meetings, afterschool programs, and extracurricular activities salient to multilingual students. To help students have an embodied experience of learning in an unknown language, a colleague and I developed a set of instructional videos in Twi, a language unknown to most students, to demonstrate different language learning methods.
