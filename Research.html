RESEARCH

Greetings! I am an Assistant Professor of Educational Leadership in the College of Education at Lewis University, where I teach courses that focus on ESL/bilingual education. My research focuses on citizenship education and language education in contexts of migration, conflict, and change, particularly in Ghana, Liberia, and the United States.

I conceptualize my underlying philosophy for engaging in educational research and teacher preparation with this Adinkra Symbol, which comes from the Akan ethnic group in Ghana. This saying “if your hands are in the dish, …,” refers to a communal style of eating from one bowl, and is a metaphor for the democratic enterprise. In order for a more equitable and healthy society for everyone, we must create schools in which 1)everyone’s hands are in the dish and 2)children are being prepared to be active participants in multiple societies.
