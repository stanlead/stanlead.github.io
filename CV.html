Professional Biography

Dr. Laura Quaynor is an Assistant Professor in the College of Education at Lewis University. She previously worked as an Assistant Professor of Curriculum and Instruction in the School of Education at the University of South Carolina Aiken in the School of Education. A former ESL and French Immersion elementary teacher, she received her Ph.D. from Emory University. Her work focuses on citizenship education in contexts of migration and conflict, particularly in West Africa and the United States. She draws on post-colonial and social justice lenses in her teaching and research, believing in the possibility that education can, indeed, lead to transformation of ourselves, or communities, and our world. She has authored recent publications in the journals Teachers' College Record, Research in Comparative and International Education, and the Journal of Peace Education. She serves on the board of the Africa Special Interest Group in the Comparative and International Education Society, as well as the Critical Educators for Social Justice Special Interest Group at AERA, and is particularly interested in considering social justice from global and transnational perspectives.
